## üõ°Ô∏è EMERGENCY PROTOCOLS

### **If Technology Fails:**
1. **Stay calm** - "Apologies for the technical difficulty"
2. **Use printed slides** - continue presenting from handouts
3. **Draw on whiteboard** - sketch key formulas (w = (c√ór)^(1/T))
4. **Explain from memory** - you know this material deeply
5. **Committee will be understanding** - they care about content, not PowerPoint

### **If You Blank on a Slide:**
1. **Pause 3 seconds** - silence is okay, shows you're thinking
2. **Look at slide content** - visual cues trigger memory
3. **Describe what you see** - "This chart shows fairness improvements..."
4. **Glance at notes** - that's why presenter view exists
5. **Bridge to next slide** - "Let me connect this to the next point..."
6. **Don't apologize profusely** - brief "excuse me" then continue

### **If You Run Out of Time:**
1. **At 18 minutes:** Check slide number - should be on slide 16-17
2. **At 19 minutes:** If on slide <18, speed up or skip details
3. **At 19:30:** Jump to conclusions (slide 19)
4. **Better to end on time than rush through** - quality over quantity

### **If You Finish Too Early (<17 minutes):**
1. **Expand on slide 11** (mechanism) - most interesting contribution
2. **Add detail to slide 8** (calibration trade-off) - core finding
3. **Slow down** - deliberate pacing shows confidence
4. **Invite early questions** - "I have time if committee has questions"

### **If Asked a Question You Can't Answer:**
**Good responses:**
- "That's an excellent question. I didn't explore that specifically, but my hypothesis would be..."
- "I don't have data on that, but it's valuable future work. I would approach it by..."
- "That's beyond the scope of my thesis, but if I were to investigate, I'd..."
- "I'm not certain, but based on my findings, I suspect..."

**Never say:**
- "I don't know" (alone - always add hypothesis or redirection)
- "That's a stupid question" (even if it is)
- "You're wrong" (to committee member)
- "I forgot" (say "I'd need to review my notes")
